          Welcome, fellow traveler.
Before anything else, my name is 
          Alberto and I'm glad you're here.
        

        

          For a long time, I've felt the pull of a new horizon. Like many of 
          you, I've been a passenger in the digital age, observing from the 
          shore — reading, learning, and dreaming about building things of my 
          own. Recently, I decided to step aboard and start the journey for 
          real.
        

        
This website is my ship's log.

        

          Here I document my journey as a nomadic indie game developer — 
          building small games, writing code, and learning the craft of 
          independent game development while living a location-independent 
          lifestyle.
        

        
Why Lumaenaut?

        

          Lumænaut ( /ˈluːmɪnɔːt/ )
Etymology: From Latin 
          lumen ("light") + Greek nautes ("sailor, traveler").
          
Meaning: A traveler of light.
        

        

          It's a word I crafted for this very voyage. It combines lumen
          — the measurement of visible light — with naut, meaning 
          traveler or voyager. In other words, someone navigating by light. 
          (And yes, I used the 'æ' character just because I love the way it 
          looks; it feels like a small spark inside the word).
        

        

          Lumaenaut is where I explore the intersection of three things that 
          fascinate me most:
        

        

          Indie Game Development: Building small games as a 
          solo developer — experimenting with mechanics, learning game design, 
          and documenting the process through dev logs.
        

        

          Coding and Computer Science: The engine room behind 
          everything. Algorithms, programming, and the tools that allow us to 
          build digital worlds.
        

        

          The Nomadic Developer Life: Designing a life where 
          code can travel with you — working remotely, building games from 
          anywhere, and exploring new places along the way.
        

        

          This site is both a developer journal and a digital nomad logbook. 
          Here I share devlogs from my indie game development journey, lessons 
          from building small and mobile games, programming experiments, tools 
          for coding while traveling, and reflections on becoming a solo 
          developer.
        

        

          I invite you to follow along as I navigate the currents of code, the 
          landscapes of new cities, and the infinite worlds inside our screens. 
          There will be discoveries, there will be storms, but hopefully, there 
          will always be light.
        

        
Welcome to the journey.

        
Pack your bags. The adventure starts now.

        
Alberto Del Valle

        
Indie Developer | Coder
