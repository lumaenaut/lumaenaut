Welcome to lumænaut!

        

          I'm Alberto, and this is my workshop for digital brainteasers—the
          kind you pick apart, turn over, and (most days) figure out.
        

        
        

          I'm hooked on problems that feel like riddles: an algorithm that
          bends around a corner case, a math enigma that winks at you from a
          LeetCode prompt, a game mechanic that just won't click. This
          website is my logbook of those mind‑bogglers—the ones I'm solving,
          the ones that stump me, and the satisfying "aha!" moments along the
          way.
        

        

          Expect a bit of everything from the digital riddle box: computer
          science challenges, mathematical curiosities, game‑dev tinkering,
          and the occasional STEM head‑scratcher that wanders into my orbit.
          I'm not here to lecture from a podium; I'm here to sit down with a
          good brainteaser, turn the pieces over, and scribble out the messy,
          fun process of making them fit.
        

        
Why lumænaut you ask?

        

          lumænaut ( /ˈluːmɪnɔːt/ )
Etymology: From Latin 
          lumen ("light") + Greek nautes ("sailor, traveler").
          
Meaning: A traveler of light.
        

        

          Untangling a knotty problem feels exactly like that—chasing beams of
          insight, moving from the dark of not‑knowing toward the glow of an
          idea. (And yes, the 'æ' is just there to give a tiny spark to the name.)
        

        

          Here's the terrain we'll cover:
        

        

          Algorithms & Data Structures: Cracking LeetCode
          challenges, optimizing for elegance, and learning why the hash map
          always saves the day.
        

        

          Mathematics: Playing with the whimsical side of
          numbers, proofs, and patterns—whether it's a Project Euler
          brain‑tickler or a geometry thought experiment.
        

        

          Game Dev: Building tiny interactive enigmas and
          mechanics, one prototype at a time.
        

        

          STEM Miscellany: Physics simulations, engineering
          teasers, and whatever else sparks curiosity.
        

        

          This isn't a textbook. It's a journal full of rough edges, dead
          ends, and the occasional eureka moment scrawled in the margins.
        

        

          Ready to solve something? Let's pull the first piece out of the box.
        

        
Alberto Del Valle

        
Coder | Indie Game Dev | Blogger
